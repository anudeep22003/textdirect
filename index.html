We are a group of five students, coming from highly diverse backgrounds, who met in graduate school at Yale University. We came together to think of possible solutions to reduce the prevalence of preventable diseases in the developing world.

We came up with the idea of TextDirect and secured fudning from D-prize and Yale University to pilot our idea in Western Africa.

To improve drug adherence rates and thus reduce the prevaence of treatable diseases and improve the standards of living of the local society.

Improving drug adherence rates has 3 direct impacts:

  • reduce the recurrence of the disease
  • prevent the disease from developing resistance to the drug
  • reduce government spending on subsidizing drugs

    • We are currently in the process of operationalizing the pilot in Sierra Leone. We have built the support of local hospitals, doctors, health workers and patients. We are also currently testing the scalable SMS platform that will be used to collect patient information and send SMS reminders to diagnosed patients.

    We launch on July 14th, 2016.
